    Six Shooter

    A Western themed, VR, wave-based shooter where the player must quickly draw and utilize their revolver to survive.

    Roles

    Designer • Programmer • Artist

    Technical Stack

    Overview

    Six Shooter is an in-progress VR game that I am developing completely on my own. The game has two modes, a target practice mode and a wave mode. In the target practice mode, the player can improve their aiming skills, while in the wave mode, the player must use their bullets, positioning, upgrades, and quick reflexes to survive.

    As the game is still in development, I am constantly adding new features and improvements to the game. I am also working on optimizing the game for VR, as well as adding new levels and enemies.

    Showcase

    Gameplay

    The game is designed to be a hectic, action-packed, and challenging experience. The game is designed to be difficult, but fair, and encourages the player to improve their skills and strategies over time. With various levels, enemies, and a set of randomized upgrades, the game is designed to be replayable and engaging.

    Visuals

    Currently, most of the assets are placeholders, as I am focusing on developing the core mechanics of the game. However, I am also working on creating new assets and improving the visuals of the game.

    Instead of trying to make the game look realistic, I am aiming for a PS1 aesthetic, with low-poly models a limited color-palette, and a low resolution. This aesthetic helps to give the game a unique look and feel, while also allowing me to create assets quickly and efficiently.

    Sound

    Six Shooter has a variety of sound effects and music that help to immerse the player in the game. The game has a multiple soundtracks that play during the game, as well sound effects for shooting, reloading, and enemy attacks.

    All of the sound effects and music are designed to be fitting for the game's western theme, and help to create a cohesive and engaging experience.

    Additional music and sounds are planned to be added in the future.

    Challenges

    One of the biggest challenges of developing Six Shooter is optimizing the game for VR. VR games require a high frame rate and low latency to be comfortable and enjoyable to play. As a result, I am constantly working on optimizing the game to run as smoothly as possible on a variety of headsets.

    Creating a game that is both challenging and fair, while also being engaging and replayable is a tall task, but it is one that I am constantly working on. The game is designed to be difficult, but it is important that the game is not frustrating. As a result, I am constantly tweaking the game's difficulty, as well as adding new features and mechanics to keep the game engaging and fun to play.

    With me being the sole developer of the game, I am also responsible for creating all of the assets, sound effects, and music for the game. This is a time-consuming task, but it also allows me to have complete creative control over the game's look and feel, resulting in a product that is truly my own.
